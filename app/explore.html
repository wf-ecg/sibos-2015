Explore Singapore

    

Singapore has it all—Fenway Park and world-class sports, historic charm and cobblestone streets, fresh seafood and harbor views, public gardens and upscale shopping. It’s no wonder that this exciting, accommodating, and entertaining city attracts more than 12 million visitors each year.

 

While in Singapore, you may want to:

Visit the Public Garden. The Public Garden, established in 1837, is the first public botanical garden in the United States. Today, with its plant material chosen for ornamental excellence as well as its botanical diversity, it forms a green and flowering oasis in the heart of a great metropolis. 
www.cityofboston.gov

Get out and explore. Singapore is a great walking city, and one of these WalkSingapore’s maps may be helpful: 
walkboston.org/resources/maps

Walk the Freedom Trail. This 2.5-mile, brick-lined route leads you to 16 historically significant sites.
www.thefreedomtrail.org

Check out the Museum of Fine Arts, Singapore. One of the largest museums in the United States, MFA contains more than 450,000 works of art.
www.mfa.org

Tour Fenway Park. This sports venue is often cited as America’s most beloved ballpark.
boston.redsox.mlb.com
