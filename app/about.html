About Wells Fargo

  

One of the largest banks in the United States, Wells Fargo is the most valuable bank in the world in terms of market capitalization. Founded in 1852 and headquartered in San Francisco, Wells Fargo provides banking, insurance, investments, mortgage, consumer, and commercial finance through various distribution channels. We maintain offices in more than 35 countries to support customers who conduct business in the global economy.

Strength and stability

Wells Fargo serves one in three businesses in the United States. We’ve operated under the same vision for decades: We want to satisfy all our customers’ financial needs and help them succeed financially.

Our capital strength, stable business model, and talented and experienced management teams are key elements that have contributed to our success and have allowed us to help our customers for over 160 years. 

The Global Financial Institutions team executes this vision by serving the increasing financial needs of U.S. and international financial institutions and public sector entities (central banks, sovereign wealth funds, multilaterals, and government agencies). GFI actively seeks to broaden and deepen our customer relationships by providing integrated, end-to-end financial solutions to our customers across multiple product platforms.
